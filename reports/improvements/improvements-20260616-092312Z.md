## 2. Must-fix

### Finding: A3 §4.2 intermittent failure — `measuring-engagement-coverage`

**Root cause.** The `measuring-engagement-coverage` skill already prohibits credit-adjacent disclaimers in the closing ("no '…not a creditworthiness judgment' line — credit vocab even in negation trips §4.2"). However, it gives the model only a negative rule with no concrete safe alternative. When the model reaches the end of an engagement-coverage readout and has no template for a closing sentence, it occasionally improvises a disclaimer — something like "this does not constitute a creditworthiness assessment" — that contains forbidden terms, tripping the §4.2 scanner. This pattern appeared in two separate prior runs and therefore qualifies as recurring.

**Whether real or artifact.** The eval's own "honest limits" note that the §4.2 scanner has a known use-vs-mention false-positive mode. However: (a) A2 — whose outputs also contain the word "covenant" and similar — has never triggered §4.2, so the scanner is not hair-trigger on its own; (b) the A3 skill's outputs in *passing* runs close with fact-boundary sentences ("Gaps computed from the activity log only — off-log contact would shorten any of these figures"), suggesting the failing runs had something materially different. The most parsimonious explanation is that in the failing runs the model added an explicit disclaimer to be thorough and that disclaimer contained a prohibited term. **Judgment: likely a real behavior problem, not purely a scanner artifact.** The fix is valid in either case.

**Proposed skill change — `centerline-plugin/skills/measuring-engagement-coverage/SKILL.md`.**

In the Rules bullet that currently reads:

> "do not write your own reliability footer or compliance disclaimer (no 'Reliability: High/Moderate' scale, no '…not a creditworthiness judgment' line — credit vocab even in negation trips §4.2). Pass the log-completeness caveat as a structured `low_confidence_input` …"

Append the following sentence immediately after that bullet:

> "To close the inline readout after presenting all borrower rows, use a factual data-boundary sentence only — e.g. *'Gaps computed from the activity log only; off-log contact would shorten any of these figures. Annotate where relevant. Requires RM review (§4.3).'* No additional sentence is needed. Anything beyond the data-boundary note and the tool's footer is surplus and risks a §4.2 hit."

**Compliance rationale (§4.2).** §4.2 bars the model from generating anything readable as a creditworthiness characterization, including negations of it. Adding a prescribed safe closing sentence gives the model a positive target, reducing the chance it reaches for a disclaimer phrased around the forbidden concepts.

**Why this does NOT weaken any guard or test.** The change is purely additive guidance in a SKILL.md. It does not touch `screen_and_finalize`, `guards.py`, or any eval case. The §4.2 scanner continues to enforce the rule; the skill change only improves the model's compliance with the rule the scanner is already testing.
