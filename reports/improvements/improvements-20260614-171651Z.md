Without that specific anchor in the skill the model reaches for an explanatory sentence, and a negation that names the forbidden word trips the §4.2 scanner just as badly as an affirmative use.

**This is a real behavior problem, not an eval false positive.** The model wrote "creditworthiness" in its own narration (the reliability-footer prose), not in a quoted block or a disclaimer about a block. The scanner correctly caught it.

**Proposed change — `measuring-engagement-coverage/SKILL.md`, Rules section.**

Add one bullet after the existing "Present the gap as a fact (§4.2)" bullet:

> - **Don't explain exclusions or rankings with credit vocabulary — even in negation.** When narrating why a lower-signal borrower sits at the bottom (e.g. "Crestwood — 0 signals, 46-day gap"), state the objective metrics and stop. Do **not** add prose such as "not a creditworthiness judgment" or "excluded on factual grounds, not a credit assessment" — a sentence that names the forbidden term, even to deny it, trips the §4.2 screen and adds nothing. The absence of credit language in the output is the compliance signal. See `screening-and-gating-output` ("Don't editorialize your own compliance").

**Compliance rationale (§4.2).** The existing "present as fact" rule is correct but not specific enough to prevent the meta-disclaimer pattern in ranking-explanation prose. Adding this wording closes the gap without changing any underlying logic.

**Why this does NOT weaken any guard or test.** The change adds a behavioral constraint (stricter output discipline). It does not touch `guards.py`, `core.py`, the MCP tools, or the eval answer key. It reinforces an existing §4.2 rule at a more specific location.
