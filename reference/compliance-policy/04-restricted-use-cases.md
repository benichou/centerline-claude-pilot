# Section 4 — Restricted Use Cases (Require Approval)

The following use cases require written approval from the CCO before deployment, even using approved tools:

## 4.1 Automated monitoring and alerting

Any system that automatically monitors borrower data and generates alerts, flags, or recommendations for RM action must be reviewed and approved. This includes:
- Automated covenant breach detection.
- Contact frequency monitoring.
- Financial trend analysis with automated output.

**Rationale:** Automated monitoring systems that generate credit-relevant signals may constitute credit analysis under Regulation B. They also create documentation obligations and may affect credit decision records.

## 4.2 Credit-adjacent outputs

AI tools may **NOT** be used to:
- Generate credit recommendations (approve, decline, downgrade, upgrade).
- Produce content that characterizes a borrower's creditworthiness.
- Recommend covenant modifications, waivers, or credit structure changes.
- Generate content that could be interpreted as a credit decision or material influence on one.

**This restriction applies to the format of the output, not just its content.** An AI-generated memo that says "Based on the financial data, this borrower presents elevated risk" — even if framed as information — may constitute a credit recommendation. When in doubt, the RM must author the credit assessment language themselves.

**AI tools MAY be used to:** Organize and present financial data, draft narrative around RM-authored assessments, flag missing information, summarize communications — as long as the credit judgment language originates with the RM.

## 4.3 Borrower-facing AI-generated content

Any content generated by AI that will be delivered to borrowers must be reviewed by the RM before sending. AI-generated content may not be sent to borrowers without human review. The RM is responsible for the accuracy and appropriateness of all communications.
