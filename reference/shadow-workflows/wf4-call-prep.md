# Workflow 4 — Call Prep Brief

This is newer — started in February. Before any important client call, I paste in my notes and recent emails and ask it to prepare me.

**Prompt (verbatim):**
```
I have a [type of call] with [borrower description] in 30 minutes.

Recent context:
[paste last 2-3 log entries and any recent emails]

Prepare a brief with:
1. What they probably want from this call
2. What I need to find out
3. 3-5 specific questions I should ask
4. Any risks or sensitivities I should be aware of
5. How to open the call

Keep it under a page. Be direct — I don't need preamble.
```

This one has genuinely changed how I prepare. The Whitmore renewal meeting — I used this prompt the morning before and it surfaced the question "what's your timeline for a decision" which I wouldn't have thought to ask explicitly. When Whitmore said 2 weeks I was ready.
