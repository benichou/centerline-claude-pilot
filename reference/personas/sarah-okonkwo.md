# Sarah Okonkwo

**Title:** Relationship Manager, Commercial Banking · **Tenure:** 9 years
**Portfolio:** 62 active relationships | $148M in total commitments
**Specialty:** Industrial manufacturing, logistics, and supply chain

## How She Works
Sarah's day starts at 7:15am. Before she opens email, she reviews her handwritten tracking sheet — a legal pad she's maintained for years that lists every borrower, last contact date, next covenant due date, and any open items. She updates it in pen every morning. It lives in the top left drawer of her desk.

She processes email in two blocks: 7:30–9:00am and after lunch. She has 847 unread emails in a subfolder called "FYI" that she's never gone back to. Her sent folder is meticulously organized by borrower name. She types faster than almost anyone on the team.

A typical Monday morning: review weekend emails (20–30 minutes), update the legal pad, pull up three to five borrower files in nCino to check on open items, prepare for any calls scheduled that week. She spends about 40% of her time on documentation and administrative work that she considers essential but not high-value.

**Her most painful task:** writing relationship review memos. "It takes me three to four hours because I care about getting the words right and I know Marcus reads them carefully. I rewrite the risk section at least twice every time."

**Her second most painful task:** tracking down overdue covenant packages. "I send the same email, wait a week, send it again, wait another week. It's just time."

## Relationship with Technology
Sarah uses: Outlook, Excel (her tracking spreadsheet is a masterpiece of manual logic), nCino (the bank's CRM/loan system — she finds it slow and clunky), Word for memos. She does not use any AI tools currently.

She tried the bank's earlier AI chatbot pilot two years ago. "It gave me wrong information about a covenant term and I had to spend two hours correcting what I'd already sent to a client. After that I didn't trust it."

She is not opposed to Claude in principle. She's opposed to broken tools. "If it actually saves me time, I'll use it. If it creates work for me to fix its mistakes, I won't."

**Her biggest concern about the adoption program:** "I don't want something that flags my contact frequency to management. I know my clients better than any dashboard does. If I haven't called Summit in three weeks it's because I spoke to Nina at a conference and there's nothing pressing. A system won't know that."

**What would make her an advocate:** One workflow that demonstrably saves her 30 minutes on a real task she does at least weekly. "If you can give me my Monday morning setup in 10 minutes instead of 45, I'm in. But it has to actually work."

**Her hidden strength:** Sarah is a natural teacher. When she adopts something, she explains it to everyone around her. If she becomes a believer, she'll train half the team without being asked.
