# Tom DeLuca

**Title:** Relationship Manager, Commercial Banking · **Tenure:** 6 years
**Portfolio:** 44 active relationships | $112M in total commitments
**Specialty:** Financial services, commercial real estate development, professional services

## How He Works
Tom describes himself as "always looking for a better way." He reads industry publications, attends fintech webinars on his own time, and has opinions about tools he's never used. He is the person other RMs come to when they have a tech question.

His workflow is less structured than Sarah's. He works in bursts — highly productive periods punctuated by periods of distraction. His Outlook inbox is a single folder with 4,200 emails, all unread. He finds things through search. "The search function is basically my filing system."

He spends roughly 25% of his time on documentation, which is lower than his peers. He attributes this to AI tools. "I'm not doing the same amount of work less — I'm doing better work in less time. The memos I write now are more thorough than the ones I wrote a year ago because I have the time to think instead of type."

**His most valuable task:** client discovery and relationship development. "The part where I'm talking to a client and figuring out what they actually need — that's what I'm good at and what the bank pays me to do. Everything else is overhead."

## Relationship with Technology
Tom uses: Outlook, ChatGPT Plus (personal account — currently under compliance review), Excel (basic), nCino (reluctantly). He is the only RM currently using AI tools.

He is aware his ChatGPT usage created a compliance issue. He is cooperative but privately frustrated. "I built something that works and now I have to stop using it because of a policy that didn't exist when I started. I understand the reason but it's still frustrating."

He is genuinely excited about Claude for Work. "If I can do what I was doing in ChatGPT but with enterprise data protections, that's strictly better. I want to be involved in building this — I've already figured out a lot of what doesn't work."

**His biggest concern about the adoption program:** "That it'll get designed by people who don't do the job. The bank's previous tool rollouts have all been built for the CCO's reporting needs, not for what the RM actually does every morning."

**His value in the adoption program:** Tom has four months of real-world prompt development and workflow design. His shadow workflows are a starting point, not a liability. The compliance issues are fixable; the insights aren't reproducible without months of trial and error.

**His risk in the adoption program:** If Tom is positioned as "the guy who got caught using ChatGPT," other RMs will distance themselves from the program. He needs to be positioned as the power user who is now doing it right, not the cautionary tale.
